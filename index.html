Eric's super short basic guide to get you started with Github. All that you need to upload your website to Github, but were too afraid to ask

Step 1

Create an Github account

  • - Go to github
  • - click on "Sign up"
  • - Enter your email address
  • - Click on continue
  • - Enter a strong password
  • - click on continue
  • - Enter your username
  • - Now you can check the checkbox for receiving occasional product updates and annoucements. You can also choose to leave it unchecked.
  • - Next step is to verify your account. For this you need to solve a puzzle.
  • - Click on verify
  • - When you'se solved the puzzle, you can click on "create account"
  • - Now you need to type the "launch code" You'll receive this code on the email address you've used to set up this account.
  • - When you've filled out the launch code you get the option to personalize your account. You can also skip this step completely.
  • - In the meantime you'll receive an email from Github to get you started.
  • - You can use the Free version perfectly for uploading your website.

Step 2

Upload your first website to Github

  • - On the left your screen you see "Create your first repository", click that.
  • - You are now in a page called "Create a new repository"
  • - You see your username followed by a /
  • - After this / you can fill in the name of your project.
  • - A little lower, you can choose between "public" and "private" Click "Public" when you want to show your website to other people.
    • - Ok, you're now ready on this page.
  • - In the lower right corner of this page you can click on "Create Repository"
  • - On the next page, you see the words "Quick setup" Below that you can read "get started by creating a new file, or uploading an existing file.
  • - Click on "uploading an existing file"
  • - On the next page you can upload your files, like index.html and styles.ss. You can just drag and drop them, or click on "Choose your files"
  • - When you're done selecting your files, you can then add something like "Initial upload" and you can add an optional description, like "made changes in index.html"

Step 3

Now what?

After a minute or so, your website will be uploaded to your github space.

You can check your site like this:

  • - When you're on the mainpage of your github, click on "setting"
  • - Then click on "pages" You then see the url of your website, and also the button "Visit site"
  • - Click on "visit site"
  • - There you go!

    • Happy coding fellow Bootcampers!
